## What This Is and Why It's Needed

The west irrigation main pipe runs N-S at ~X=192, entering the yard from the north. The North Patio Extension (Y=105~225) will be paved over this area. Once the zone has 4" of compacted base + sand + pavers, the pipe is permanently buried.

A **conduit sleeve** is a 4" PVC pipe installed in the ground *before* the base course is laid. The irrigation pipe runs through the inside of the sleeve. If the pipe ever breaks or needs upgrading, pull it out from either end — no need to break up the patio.

**Head adjustment** is done at the same time: 2 heads in the north zone (now under paving) are capped, and 2 remaining heads get 180° half-circle nozzles to maintain lawn coverage.

## Step-by-Step

### Step 1 — Call 811
Call 811 at least 3 business days before digging. Required by Colorado law even for your own irrigation pipe — other utilities may cross the same zone.

### Step 2 — Locate the west pipe
- Turn on the irrigation system briefly and probe the soil at ~X=192 to find the pipe
- Mark its exact path from Y=105 to Y=225 with flags every 3 ft

### Step 3 — Dig a trench along the pipe
- Trench width: ~8" (enough to expose the pipe and lay the sleeve beside it)
- Trench depth: match the pipe's existing burial depth (typically 6–12")
- Dig only within the future patio footprint: Y=105 to Y=225
- Hand-dig the last few inches once you're near the pipe to avoid cutting it

### Step 4 — Expose the pipe entry/exit points
- At Y=105 (north end): uncover the pipe where it exits the patio footprint heading toward the north fence
- At Y=225 (south end): uncover the pipe where it exits into the existing patio zone
- You only need to disconnect at ONE end — the north end is easiest (least soil depth)

### Step 5 — Cut and disconnect the pipe at the north end
- Cut the pipe cleanly just north of Y=105 (outside the patio zone)
- Keep the cut-off section — you'll reconnect it

### Step 6 — Lay the sleeve
- Cut the 4" PVC to length (~15 ft) — join with a coupling + primer/cement if needed for two 10 ft sections
- Lay the sleeve in the trench along the same path as the pipe
- Leave 2–3" of sleeve protruding at each end (north and south) so the caps are accessible above final grade after backfill
- The sleeve does not need to be glued to the end caps — they should remain hand-removable

### Step 7 — Thread the pipe through the sleeve
- Apply dish soap or pipe lubricant to the irrigation pipe
- Feed the south end of the pipe into the north end of the sleeve and push it through
- The pipe is flexible poly — 15 ft through 4" PVC feeds easily with one person

### Step 8 — Reconnect the pipe
- At the north end (outside patio): reconnect the cut pipe using a barbed coupler + 2 hose clamps
- Tighten clamps firmly; turn on irrigation to test for leaks before backfilling

### Step 9 — Cap the sleeve ends
- Thread on both end caps hand-tight
- Mark the cap locations on your site plan so you know where they are post-paving

### Step 10 — Backfill the trench
- Backfill in lifts, tamping lightly — do not compact directly over the sleeve
- The patio base course will be laid over this zone in a later step

### Step 11 — Head adjustment
- Locate and cap the 2 northernmost sprinkler heads (now inside the future patio footprint)
- Unscrew the nozzle on 2 remaining heads; replace with 180° half-circle nozzles
- Run the irrigation system and confirm coverage of the retained lawn zone (X=192~468, Y=160~385)
